ABSTRACT 
 
College life is full of changes, and students often find themselves with things they no 
longer need—books from last semester, gadgets they’ve outgrown, or furniture that 
won’t fit in their next rental. At the same time, other students are looking for these very 
items at affordable prices. College Dealz bridges this gap by creating a student-
exclusive reselling platform where buyers and sellers can connect easily, securely, and 
quickly. 
 
Unlike general online marketplaces, College Dealz is tailor-made for university 
students. By verifying users through university email IDs, it ensures a trusted 
environment and drastically reduces the chances of scams or spam. It further simplifies 
the reselling experience with features like category-wise search, price-based filtering, 
real-time chat, instant notifications, and a responsive UI—eliminating the need to rely 
on disorganized social media groups or messaging platforms. 
 
From a technical perspective, the project leverages a robust tech stack: 
 Frontend: Built using React.js, providing a smooth and dynamic user 
experience. 
 Backend: Developed with Spring Boot, ensuring secure, scalable, and 
maintainable server-side logic. 
 Database: MySQL is used for reliable and structured data storage. 
 Real-Time Chat: Integrated using WebSockets for seamless buyer-seller 
communication. 
  
To support a scalable and production-ready environment, the project utilizes various 
AWS services: 
 Amazon RDS for hosting the MySQL database in the cloud with high 
availability. 
 Amazon EC2 to deploy and manage the backend services. 
 Amazon S3 for fast and secure image storage, replacing slower local blob 
handling. These integrations ensure better performance, reliability, and speed 
across all parts of the platform. 
 
Developed by a dedicated two-person team following agile methodologies, the work 
was split across frontend and backend in parallel, allowing efficient development and 
continuous iteration. Hosting and CI/CD were handled via platforms like Vercel (for 
frontend) and Railway (during development phase), with production deployments 
now on AWS for better control and scalability. 
 
At its core, College Dealz is not just a project—it’s a student-centric solution that 
enhances campus life. It helps students save money, reduce waste, and connect with 
their local community, all while gaining real-world experience in using modern tools 
and technologies. Whether someone is searching for a budget-friendly laptop or trying 
to give a second life to their old textbooks, College Dealz makes the process simple, 
secure, and truly student-first.

1. INTRODUCTION 
 
1.1 PROJECT DETAILS 
College Dealz is a reselling platform specifically designed for university students. It 
allows students to buy and sell second-hand items within their university community. 
The platform ensures a smooth and secure experience by offering features like user 
authentication, product search and filtering, and real-time chat. 
Students often accumulate items such as books, electronics, furniture, and accessories 
that they no longer need. Instead of throwing these items away or struggling to find 
buyers through social media, College Dealz provides a structured marketplace where 
students can easily post listings and connect with potential buyers. 
 
1.2 PURPOSE 
The purpose of College Dealz is to create a student-centered marketplace that makes 
buying and selling second-hand items more convenient, affordable, and accessible. The 
key benefits include: 
• Cost Savings: Students can buy items at lower prices compared to retail stores. 
• Sustainability: Encouraging reuse reduces waste and promotes eco -friendly 
habits. 
• Community-Based Transactions: The platform ensures buyers and sellers are 
university students, making transactions safer and more reliable. 
• Convenience: Students can easily find, compare, and negotiate deals without 
leaving campus. 
 
1.3 SCOPE 
1.3.1 User Scope 
• Sellers: University students who want to sell their used or unwanted 
items. 
• Buyers: Students looking for affordable, second-hand products. 
• Administrators: A team managing the platform, handling 
complaints, and verifying listings. 
 
1.3.2 Functional Scope 
• User Registration & Authentication: Users can sign up using their 
university email for verification. 
• Product Listing & Management: Sellers can add details like price, 
images, and descriptions of items. 
• Search & Sorting Features: Users can filter listings based on 
category, price, and location.

3. SYSTEM REQUIREMENT STUDY 
 
3.1 STUDY OF CURRENT SYSTEM 
 
Currently, university students lack a dedicated platform to conveniently buy and sell 
second-hand goods such as textbooks, electronics, and daily essentials. Many rely on 
general online marketplaces like OLX or Facebook Marketplace, which, while 
functional, are not tailored to student needs. 
 
These platforms present several challenges: 
• Trust Issues: Buyers and sellers are often strangers, raising concerns about 
fraud and reliability. 
• Inconvenient Transactions: Students may need to travel across the city to 
meet sellers, which is time-consuming and impractical given academic 
schedules. 
• Scattered Listings: There is no centralized place for student-specific deals, 
making it difficult to find relevant listings efficiently. 
College Dealz  is designed to solve these issues by providing a community-driven 
marketplace exclusively for university students, ensuring trust, convenience, and 
security in every transaction. 
 
3.2    PROBLEMS AND WEAKNESSES OF CURRENT SYSTEM 
The existing approach of using general reselling platforms poses multiple challenges: 
1. Lack of a Trusted Community 
o No verification process ensures that buyers and sellers are genuine 
students. 
o Risk of scams, overpriced listings, or misleading product descriptions. 
2. Inconvenience and Time Consumption 
o Finding the right product requires searching across multiple platforms. 
o Arranging meet-ups with unknown sellers can be difficult and risky. 
3. Lack of Student-Specific Features 
o No dedicated filters for student essentials like textbooks, student items, 
or course-specific gadgets. 
4. Security Concerns 
o No built-in messaging system, leading users to share personal contact 
details. 
o No safeguards against fake or misleading listings. 
 
College Dealz aims to eliminate these issues by creating a secure, student-only 
marketplace with built-in verification, localized deals, and smart filters tailored to 
student needs.

9. LIMITATION AND FUTURE ENHANCEMENT 
While College Dealz serves its core purpose well—connecting students within 
university communities for buying and selling items—there are still a few limitations 
in its current version that offer great opportunities for future improvements. 
9.1 CURRENT LIMITATIONS 
9.2.1 Basic Wantlist Matching 
 At present, when a buyer adds an item to their wantlist and a seller posts a 
matching product, the system triggers a notification only if the product is 
accessed via the wantlist notification. 
 This restricts automatic notifications from other product sources like profile 
reposts or direct post-a-deal forms. 
 
9.2.2 No Real-Time Notification Update 
 Notifications are currently queued and displayed on refresh or fetching after 
few intervals.  
 Users might miss immediate updates unless they actively check their 
dashboard or reload the page. 
 
9.2.3 No Integrated Payment Tracking 
 Transactions are entirely manual. The platform does not track if a product 
was actually paid for or just marked as sold, which reduces platform-level 
transparency and trust metrics. 
 
9.2.4 No Product Authenticity Verification 
 There's currently no verification system in place to ensure that the product 
listed matches its description or photos. 
 
9.2.5 Limited Analytics for Admin 
 Admin dashboard lacks detailed statistics such as most active universities, 
average product lifespan, most wanted items, etc., which could help in 
improving platform decisions 
. 
9.2.6 No Support for Group Chats 
 The chat system is limited to 1-on-1 conversations between buyer and seller. 
Group discussions, student community alerts, or broadcast messages are not 
yet supported. 
 
9.2.7 Fixed University Association 
 Currently, users are tightly bound to a single university. All product listings, 
chats, and notifications are scoped strictly within that university domain. 
 
9.2.8 Lack of Flexibility in User Grouping 
 Users cannot join or view other communities even if they belong to multiple 
institutions (e.g., alumni, cross-college groups, or city-based collectives).

10.2.2  Problems Encountered and Possible Solutions 
Throughout development, we encountered a range of technical, architectural, and 
collaboration-related challenges. 
 
10.2.1.1 Frontend (React.js) Issues 
 Problem: State not persisting across pages (e.g., Dark Mode toggle) 
    Solution:   Used React Context API to create a global theme state. 
 
 Problem: Frequent re-renders and uncleaned effects. 
Solution: Optimized components using React Hooks like useMemo, 
                   useCallback, and useEffect cleanup functions. 
 
 Problem: Chat not live or reactive. 
   Solution: Integrated WebSocket with STOMP protocol and used SockJS 
       on the frontend for seamless real-time messaging. 
 
 Problem: Too many products made the homepage laggy. 
   Solution: Introduced pagination using Spring Data and frontend pagination 
       buttons. 
 
10.2.1.2 Backend (Spring Boot) Issues 
 Problem: N+1 Query fetching issue when getting user deals and feedbacks. 
   Solution: Used @Fetch (FetchType.LAZY) and DTOs with projections to  
       fetch only required data. 
 
 Problem: Circular reference error during JSON response. 
   Solution: Used @JsonIgnore, DTOs to avoid infinite recursion. 
 
 Problem: Notification model created redundant objects for each user. 
   Solution: Changed the mapping to Many-to-Many between User and  
Notification to maintain a single notification shared with many 
users. 
 
 Problem: Scaling Chat with Dynamic Users. 
   Solution: Used @EnableWebSocketMessageBroker with destination  
       prefix and session tracking 
 
 
10.2.1.3 Deployment & DevOps Challenges 
 Problem: Blob image URLs were slow to load and cache. 
Solution: Migrated image storage to AWS S3 for fast, reliable access. 
 
 Problem: Need to share a common dev database with the team. 
Solution: Used TiDB Cloud (free plan) to allow database access across     
devices and locations.
